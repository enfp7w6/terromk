Step-by-step tutorials and technical walkthroughs.

Background Removal

I used Adobe After Effects to trace the moving person and cut them out from the original background. By carefully tracking their movement frame by frame, I was able to separate them completely from the video scene. This left me with a clean cutout of the person, making it easy to drop them into a brand new background.

Blocking in Colors

After cutting out the subject, the next step is blocking in the base colors for each part of the character or object. This means separating areas like the hair, skin, and clothing into flat, solid color shapes on different layers. Doing this creates a clean foundation and makes it much easier to add detailed shading and lighting to specific sections later on without messing up the rest of the image.

Rim Light Outline

The final step is adding a bright outline highlight around the person's body to separate them from the new background. This rim light effect wraps around the edges of the moving figure, making their silhouette pop clearly against the environment. Because this outline follows the body's exact shape, it dynamically adjusts with every movement, giving the character a polished look and a strong sense of depth within the scene.
